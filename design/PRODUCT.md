# GitFut — Product Context

## Register
brand

> GitFut's landing and share surfaces ARE the product. The whole point is a card people *want* to post. Design carries the value; it isn't just serving a tool.

## Product Purpose

Enter your GitHub username, get a FIFA-Ultimate-Team-style collectible player card built from your real GitHub stats (rated out of 99, with a tier from Bronze to Legend), and share it. The deterministic scoring engine already exists; the design's job is to make the card a thing people screenshot and post to start a trend.

The single overriding goal is **shareability / virality**. Every design decision is judged by: does this make someone more likely to post their card?

## Users

Developers on GitHub — from students with a Bronze card to maintainers with an Icon. They live in dark mode, read terminals, recognize FUT/EA FC cards, and share status-flex content (Wrapped-style) on X, LinkedIn, and Instagram. They are design-literate enough to smell generic AI output and bounce.

## Brand & Tone

Premium FUT prestige **crossed with** playful dev humor. The mascot (an Octocat-as-footballer kicking the World Cup 26 ball) sets the playful register; the card and the gold set the prestige register. Confident, a little cheeky (football-git puns: "Penalty Resistance", "eXpected Greatness"), never stiff-corporate, never try-hard.

Cultural anchor: **FIFA World Cup 26** (USA/Canada/Mexico). We borrow its color palette only (black / white / gray / gold), not its IP or typeface. The mascot ball carries the real WC26 host-nation color arcs.

## Strategic Principles

1. **The card is the only hero.** Material hierarchy: the card is the single glossy, high-material object; all chrome is matte and recedes. The card wins every screen.
2. **Green is the action; gold is the prize.** GitHub-green brands every interactive/action surface (input, CTA, links, success). Gold is reserved for prestige (the Gold tier, the rating chip, the WC26 register). They never fight on the same surface.
3. **Depth from light, not glass.** Premium-on-dark is achieved with glow and spotlight, never decorative glassmorphism.
4. **Earn the screenshot, earn the click.** The reveal earns the screenshot (walkout + tier-scaled burst); the OG image earns the click on a shared link. Both are first-class.
5. **Accessible by default.** Reduced-motion is a real path, tier identity is never color-alone, contrast holds on both dark and light social feeds.

## Anti-references (what GitFut must never look like)

- Generic coral/purple dark-mode SaaS (what the original MVP looked like).
- Glassmorphism-everywhere "AI slop" dashboards.
- The hero-metric template (big number + small label + gradient).
- Identical card-grid feature sections.
- Stiff corporate tournament microsite.
- Gradient text, side-stripe accent borders, modal-first flows.

## Surfaces

Landing · Loading · Reveal · Result · Public card page (`/u/[username]`) + dynamic OG image. No leaderboards/profiles/compare in v1.
